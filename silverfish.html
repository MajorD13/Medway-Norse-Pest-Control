Silverfish: Damage-Causing Moisture Lovers

Summary: Silverfish are small, wingless insects with a silvery, metallic appearance and a fish-like shape. They are known for their quick, wriggling movements.

Habitat: Silverfish thrive in damp, humid environments, such as basements, bathrooms, kitchens, and laundry rooms. They are often found near leaky pipes or in areas with high humidity.

Why They Are Pests: Silverfish feed on starchy materials, including paper, glue, books, wallpaper, and fabrics. They can cause damage to books, documents, and clothing.

Why Use a Professional: Effective silverfish control involves reducing humidity levels, eliminating moisture sources, and using appropriate insecticides or traps. Professionals can identify the conditions that attract silverfish and recommend a comprehensive treatment and prevention plan.
