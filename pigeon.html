Bird Control (Pigeons): Dealing with Feathered Nuisances

Summary: Pigeons, are common urban birds with stout bodies and short legs. They are often grey with iridescent feathers.

Habitat: Pigeons are highly adaptable and thrive in urban environments, nesting on buildings, bridges, and other structures.

Why They Are Pests: Pigeon droppings are acidic and can damage buildings, statues, and vehicles. Their droppings also pose health risks, as they can harbour diseases like Histoplasmosis. Nesting materials can block gutters and drains, and large flocks can be a general nuisance.

Why Use a Professional: Bird control requires humane and effective methods, such as netting, spikes, and deterrents, to prevent pigeons from roosting and nesting. Professionals can assess the situation, recommend appropriate solutions, and ensure compliance with wildlife regulations.
