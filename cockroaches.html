Cockroaches: Resilient Pests and Health Hazards

Summary: Cockroaches are flat, oval-shaped insects with long antennae and a rapid, scuttling gait. Common pest species include German cockroaches and Oriental cockroaches.

Habitat: Cockroaches prefer warm, humid environments with access to food and water. They are commonly found in kitchens, bathrooms, basements, and sewers.

Why They Are Pests: Cockroaches are a major health concern, as they spread diseases (like Salmonella and Gastroenteritis), trigger allergies and asthma, and contaminate food and surfaces with their droppings and shed skin.

Why Use a Professional: Cockroaches are notoriously difficult to eradicate due to their resilience and rapid reproduction. Professionals can implement a comprehensive treatment plan, including sanitation, insecticides, and baiting, to eliminate existing cockroaches and prevent future infestations.
